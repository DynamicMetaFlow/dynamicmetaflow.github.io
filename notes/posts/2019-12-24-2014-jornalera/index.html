Jornalera

24 Dec 2019

Journaler@ is a data collection and organizing tool to combat and prevent wage theft and workplace violations.

The project’s long term goal is to enable stakeholders to increase the capacity of community based organizations to address low-wage workers’ issues.

The development of the Jornaler@ app began with the work of social practice artist Sol Aramendi and workers and leaders at the New Immigrant Community Empowerment (NICE), affiliate of NDLON.

Application was build in collaboration between The Worker Institute at Cornell and partner organizations:

  • National Day Laborer Organizing Network (NDLON)
  • American Federation of Labor-Congress of Industrial Organizations (AFL-CIO)
  • International Union of Painters and Allied Trades (IUPAT)

Features

There are three key functions:

1) Check-In / Check-Out:

Keeps track of workers hours, wages, locations, breaks and relevant employer and worksite information. Information is gathered and creates a “Job Log” displaying weekly, annual wages and average wage for jobs in area.

  • When
  • Salary
  • How Many Hours
  • Where

2) Report:

Collects information to document and begin a wage theft complaint to be delivered to Department of Labor, also asks for additional information i.e worker center, or contacting employer for mediation.

3) Alert:

Allows workers to anonymously warn others of non-paying jobs and bad employers

Center Code Model

To ensure that users are connecting with local communities of workers, the “center code” model was developed.

Those that want to use Jornaler@ can get in touch with their closest worker center. If the center is already connected to the Jornaler@ Network, they will train the worker in how to best utilize the app and distribute a “center code” to allow that worker to register.

Pros

  • Application was made in direct collaboration with members of the community, workers, organizers, artists and organizations / workercenters
  • The tool is designed in such a way that it’s not aiming to replace organizers or organizations but rather ‘increase the capcity of community based organizations to address workers’ issues
  • “Center Code” model, allows workercenters who wish to participate in the network to register and given a unique code and where workers can elect to work with that specific worker center.
  • Tracks worker hours, wages, locations, breaks, employter information, creates reports and job logs used for DOL, and can anonymously share information with others about non-paying jobs or bad employers
  • Application is cross platform available for Android and IOS devices
  • Trainings and workshops are provided for workercenters that elect to register and join the network.

Deltas

  • The code written for the application is not available to the public

  • Workers must have an existing relationship with a workercenter to be given the code that is necessary in order to use the applcation, and or “active”. Thus even if a worker found out about the application through word of mouth they couldn’t use it.

  • Dependent on workers actively logging hours and using application
  • When a worker makes first contact with an organization about a wage and hour violation the application is only useful after the worker is aware of it’s existence, has been trained of how to use it and actively uses it afterwards, does not take into account prior violations.
